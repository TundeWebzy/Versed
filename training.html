Training & support that stays with you

Versed Global designs Salesforce and fundraising training so people understand not just which buttons to press, but how your CRM and income model fit together in practice.

Training shaped around your system

Training is built on your real configuration, programmes and fundraising pipelines, rather than generic demo orgs. Sessions use your page layouts, fields, reports and flows so what staff see on‑screen matches what they log into every day.

Different teams see different journeys. Service delivery and operations teams focus on clean data entry, safeguarding considerations and programme reporting. Fundraisers focus on pipeline design, opportunity stages, tasks and stewardship workflows. Admins and “super users” go deeper into the configuration decisions that sit behind the scenes.

The result is not a one‑off demonstration, but a shared way of working: consistent approaches to records, tasks and reports that make your Salesforce environment easier to sustain and easier to trust.

Learning paths that flow with your roles

Different people need different depth. A horizontal “rail” makes it easy to see how training can roll out across your team.

Path 1
End‑users & delivery staff
Focus on the tasks they perform most often: logging interactions, updating records, managing caseloads and producing simple reports that feed funders and management.
Path 2
Fundraising & income teams
Emphasis on opportunities, pipelines, stewardship journeys and reporting that gives a clear picture of income and risk.
Path 3
Managers & SMT
Reading dashboards, asking the right questions of the data and understanding what Salesforce can and cannot yet tell you.
Path 4
Admins & “super users”
Configuration basics, change control, governance and small improvements that avoid technical debt.

Support models that flex with you

Training often works best when there is ongoing space for questions and iteration. Support can be light‑touch or more structured, depending on your internal capacity.

Advisory hours & clinics

A bank of hours gives your team somewhere to turn when they hit a question that goes beyond a simple “how‑to”. This might be a tricky report, a flows question, a fundraising workflow that feels clunky, or a proposed change that needs a second view.

Drop‑in clinics create regular slots for people to share issues and ideas. Together, you can refine processes, tidy fields, clarify responsibilities and identify training needs that emerge after the initial rollout.

Managed support & optimisation

For organisations that want a regular rhythm, a lightweight managed support arrangement can cover small configuration changes, periodic reviews of reports and dashboards, and micro‑training for new starters.

This helps you avoid the “we’ll fix it later” backlog that often builds up in Salesforce, and ensures that training materials reflect how the system actually behaves as it evolves.
