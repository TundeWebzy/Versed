Regional charity: From spreadsheets to Salesforce

Illustrative outcome: income growth with better data

Challenge

  • Service users and volunteers tracked in spreadsheets
  • No central view of grant requirements or deadlines
  • Manual reporting for multiple funders
  • Fundraising and delivery data not connected

Approach

  • Salesforce Nonprofit Success Pack implementation
  • Service delivery and volunteer management modules
  • Fundraising and grant tracking in a single CRM
  • Dashboards for operational and funder reporting

This type of project reduces manual reporting effort, improves data quality and creates a single view of service users, volunteers and funders in one Salesforce environment.

Typical benefits

Less admin time
Grant reporting prepared from CRM data
Clearer insight
Service and income data in one place
Stronger cases
Evidence for applications and renewals

National charity: Trust and foundation programme

Illustrative outcome: stronger, more predictable pipeline

Challenge

  • Low and inconsistent grant success rate
  • No systematic prospect research or prioritisation
  • Pipeline held in spreadsheets and emails
  • Salesforce used only for basic recording

Approach

  • Trust and foundation strategy and case for support
  • Prioritised multi‑year pipeline and research
  • Structured proposal templates and review support
  • Configured Salesforce opportunity stages and reports

Work of this kind typically improves success rates, supports clearer decision‑ making on where to invest time and creates a shared view of the pipeline across teams.

Typical benefits

Higher quality bids
Consistent case and evidence base
Stronger pipeline
Clear priorities over 12–36 months
Better visibility
Board‑ and SMT‑ready reporting
